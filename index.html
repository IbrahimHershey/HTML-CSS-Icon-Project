It was 7:59pm EST. August 31st. College football was finally back after nearly six months. The Florida Gators were taking on the Utah Utes. Only one more minute until kickoff. Then all of a sudden.

POOF. Everything went black.

Nobody knew what was going on. Fans were outraged thinking it was their TVs but they quickly needed to look for an alternative. Then all of a sudden a message popped up:

After a couple minutes, people started to realize what was going on. Disney and Spectrum were struggling over the terms of their distribution agreement. Both sides could not reach a deal and Disney blacked out their programming on Spectrum TVs.

As a result, nearly 15 million Spectrum subscribers lost access to ESPN, ABC, and other Disney channels; They had to wait close to three hours on hold over the weekend as they tried to cancel their Spectrum subscriptions.
